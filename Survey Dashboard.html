Key Insights

  • Counter-intuitive finding: Men rate gendered language impact higher than women (3.31 vs 2.35)
  • 92.3% specified desired tool features, showing strong behavioral demand despite mixed ratings
  • Real-time analysis (61%) and alternative suggestions (39%) are most requested features
  • Clear gender differences in word preferences: "Responsive" and "Collaborative" favor women
  • High OR experience (67.6%) validates survey relevance and credibility
  • Students (43.6%) and academia (42.1%) dominate sample, ideal for university partnership

Gender Paradox Discovery

  • Counter-intuitive: Men rate gendered language impact higher than women (3.31 vs 2.35 average)
  • Word preferences: Largest gaps in "Responsive" (Women 3.74 vs Men 3.00) and "Collaborative" (Women 3.57 vs Men 2.92)
  • Research implication: This challenges assumptions and suggests different awareness/sensitivity patterns
